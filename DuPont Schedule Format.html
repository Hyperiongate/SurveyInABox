Key Feature
The classic DuPont — a 12-hour, four-crew pattern famous for ending every cycle with a full seven-day break. Over four weeks you rotate through blocks of nights and days and average 42 hours a week.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews cover 24/7. The pattern rotates each crew through a block of nights and a block of days and staggers them so the operation stays covered — even while one crew is on its long stretch off.

2
Your four weeks

A representative cycle runs four nights, three off, three days, one off, three nights, three off, four days, then a full seven days off — 42 hours a week on average, capped by a whole week's break.
