Key Feature
A five-day, 40-hour schedule that alternates between a day week and an evening week. Weekends stay off; the only thing that changes is your start time, flipping every other week. Useful where you need coverage into the evening but not overnight.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Two crews typically run this opposite each other — while one is on days the other is on evenings, then they swap. Together they staff a day and an evening shift Monday through Friday.

2
Your two weeks

Week 1 you work five 8-hour day shifts, Monday to Friday, weekend off. Week 2 you work the same Monday-to-Friday block on evenings. Then it repeats — 40 hours every week, every weekend off.

Key Feature
Three crews cover days, evenings and nights Monday through Friday, and each crew rotates through all three over a three-week cycle. It shares the evening and night weeks evenly instead of leaving anyone permanently off-hours — still 40 hours with weekends off.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Key Feature
A 24/5 setup that keeps a permanent night crew but lets the day and evening crews oscillate between the two, so the less-popular evening slot is shared. Forty-hour weeks, weekends off, continuous Monday-to-Friday coverage.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

A fixed night crew covers overnights Monday through Friday and never rotates. The other crews split days and evenings and swap between them every other week, so no one is stuck on evenings permanently. Together they cover all 24 hours, five days a week.

2
Your weeks

On the oscillating crews you work days Monday–Friday one week and evenings the next, weekends off throughout. On the night crew you work the same overnight shift Monday–Friday every week.
