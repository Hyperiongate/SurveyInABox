Key Feature
The classic 2-2-3 (often called the Panama): work two, off two, work three, then off two, work two, off three. Its signature is that every other weekend is a full three-day weekend off, and you never work more than three days in a row. Averages 42 hours over the two-week cycle.
How It Works
1
The pattern

Across 14 days you work a 2-day block, a 2-day block and a 3-day block with matching breaks between them. The rhythm is built so your days off land on the same weekdays every two weeks and every second weekend is completely free.

2
Your two weeks

You work in short 2- and 3-day blocks — never more than three shifts in a row — for seven 12-hour shifts across the fortnight, 42 hours a week, with every other weekend fully off.

Key Feature
The 2-2-3 run as a fixed 24/7 operation — two crews on days, two on nights, none rotating. You keep the every-other-weekend-off rhythm and never work more than three days in a row, but night crews are permanent.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews cover the clock. Two work 12-hour days and two work 12-hour nights, each pair offset so that when one crew is in a 2- or 3-day block its partner is off. Nobody switches between days and nights.

2
Your two weeks

On a day crew you work the 2-2-3 rhythm on day shift — two on, two off, three on, then the mirror — seven shifts across the fortnight and 42 hours a week, with every other weekend fully off. A night crew does the same on nights.

Key Feature
The 2-2-3 with day and night work mixed in on a fast rotation, so night shifts are shared across everyone rather than falling on permanent night crews. Same every-other-weekend-off rhythm and 42-hour average.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews run the 2-2-3, but instead of staying on one shift they rotate quickly between days and nights. At any time two crews are on days and two on nights, keeping the operation covered around the clock.

2
Your cycle

You work the familiar 2-2-3 blocks, but your day and night shifts alternate through the cycle so you never spend long stretches on nights. You still average 42 hours and keep every other weekend off.

Key Feature
The 2-2-3 on a slower rotation — a longer stretch on days before switching to a longer stretch on nights. It shares night work like the rapid version but with fewer day/night changeovers, which many people find easier on the body.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews cover 24/7 on the 2-2-3, rotating between days and nights on a longer cycle than the rapid version — a run of day rotations, then a run of night rotations. Two crews are always on days and two on nights.

2
Your cycle

You work the 2-2-3 blocks but stay on days for a longer run before moving to a run of nights. Every other weekend is off and you average 42 hours a week.
