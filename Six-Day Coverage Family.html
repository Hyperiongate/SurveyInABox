Key Feature
Monday-through-Saturday coverage on 12-hour shifts, with Sundays always off. A three-week pattern gives each worker four shifts a week (48 hours) plus a second, rotating day off during the week.
How It Works
1
The pattern

The operation runs six days a week, Monday through Saturday, and closes Sunday. Over a three-week cycle your days off move around the week so the workload — and the Saturday duty — is shared evenly.

2
Your three weeks

You work four 12-hour shifts a week, Sunday always off plus one other day that shifts through the cycle — 48 hours a week across the three-week pattern.
