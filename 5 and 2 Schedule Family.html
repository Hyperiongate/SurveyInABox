Key Feature
A 12-hour pattern built on a long five-day work block plus a shorter two-day block, arranged so every other weekend is completely off. Run fixed: two crews hold days, two hold nights, and no one rotates between them.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews cover 24/7. Two are permanent day crews and two permanent night crews, offset so their long and short work blocks interlock into continuous coverage.

2
Your two weeks

You work a five-day block and a two-day block across the fortnight — seven 12-hour shifts, 42 hours a week — and every other weekend is fully off. Day crews stay on days, night crews on nights.

Key Feature
The same long-block 5 & 2 pattern, but crews rotate between days and nights every two weeks so night duty is shared. Keeps the every-other-weekend-off rhythm and the 42-hour average.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews run the 5 & 2, rotating between day and night shifts on a two-week cycle. One crew starts in week one and its partner in week three, offset so coverage stays continuous while everyone takes a turn on nights.

2
Your rotation

You work the five-and-two blocks on days for two weeks, then the same blocks on nights for two weeks, and so on. Every other weekend is off and you average 42 hours a week.
