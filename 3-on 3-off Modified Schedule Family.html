Key Feature
The 3-on/3-off rhythm with one shift relocated so every weekend is whole — you never work just Saturday or just Sunday. The trade is one shorter (2-in-a-row) and one longer (4-in-a-row) work block; the 42-hour average is unchanged.
How It Works
1
The pattern

It's the standard three-on, three-off rhythm, but a single Saturday shift is moved within the cycle to erase the split weekends. The result: when you're off on a weekend you get both days, and when you work one you work both.

2
Your six weeks

You still work three-day blocks for the most part, with one two-day and one four-day block where the shift was moved. Every weekend is now clean, and you average 42 hours a week.

The clean-weekend pattern run by four fixed crews. Each paired crew starts three weeks later, so days and nights interlock into unbroken 24/7 coverage — and every crew still gets whole weekends.

Key Feature
The clean-weekend 3-on/3-off run as a fixed 24/7 operation — two day crews, two night crews, no rotation. Every crew keeps whole weekends, and coverage interlocks because the partner crew starts three weeks into the cycle.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews cover the clock, two on days and two on nights. Each pair runs the clean-weekend pattern offset by three weeks so their blocks interlock into continuous coverage. Nobody switches between days and nights.

2
Your six weeks

You work the modified three-on, three-off on your fixed shift, with whole weekends and a 42-hour average. Your partner crew fills every gap you leave.
