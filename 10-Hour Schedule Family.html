Key Feature

How It Works
1
Where the crews go

2
Your week

You work ten hours a day, Monday through Thursday, and are off Friday, Saturday and Sunday. Every week is the same.

Key Feature
Two crews on 10-hour days, offset by a day — Crew A works Monday–Thursday, Crew B works Tuesday–Friday. That doubles your staffing midweek, when it's busiest, and runs lighter on Monday and Friday. Everyone works 40 hours with weekends off.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Crew A takes Monday through Thursday; Crew B takes Tuesday through Friday. On Monday only Crew A is in and on Friday only Crew B, so those days run at half staff; Tuesday through Thursday both crews overlap for full staffing. Neither crew works weekends.

2
Your week

On Crew A you work Monday–Thursday; on Crew B you work Tuesday–Friday. Either way it's four 10-hour days, 40 hours, with a three-day weekend.

Key Feature
Five sub-crews, each with a different weekday off, so four of the five are always present — steady, balanced staffing five days a week. Everyone works 40 hours and, over a five-week cycle, rotates through every day-off position so the Friday long weekend is shared fairly.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

The workforce is split into five sub-crews. In any given week each has a different weekday off — one is off Monday, one Tuesday, on to Friday — so four of the five are working every weekday. Weekends are off for everyone.

2
Your five weeks

Your day off marches back through the week — Friday off in week 1, Thursday in week 2, on to Monday in week 5 — then repeats. Every week is four 10-hour days for 40 hours, and over the cycle you get the Friday-Saturday-Sunday long weekend once, the same as everyone else.
