Key Feature
Four 12-hour shifts on, four off — the simplest long-block continuous rhythm. It averages 42 hours a week, but because the 8-day cycle slides against the 7-day week it takes eight weeks before your days off return to the same weekdays.
How It Works
1
The pattern

You work four days, take four off, and repeat. Shown on a calendar the four-day block walks later each week, so your weekends off drift through the cycle.

2
Your eight weeks

Every block is four 12-hour shifts, then four days off — 48-hour and 36-hour weeks that average to 42. After eight weeks the pattern realigns with the same weekdays.

Key Feature
The 4-on/4-off with day and night work folded in — two days, two nights, four off — so night shifts are shared and no one is permanently nocturnal. Still averages 42 hours and realigns every eight weeks.
How It Works

Where the crews go

Your eight weeks

Key Feature
The 4-on/4-off run as a fixed 24/7 operation — two day crews, two night crews, none rotating. The partner crew starts four weeks in so the long blocks interlock into continuous coverage.
How It Works
1
Where the crews go

Four crews cover the clock, two permanently on days and two on nights. Each pair runs four-on/four-off offset by four weeks so their blocks interlock. Nobody switches shift.

2
Your eight weeks

You work four 12-hour shifts on your fixed shift, then four days off, and repeat — averaging 42 hours a week while your partner crew covers your days off.
